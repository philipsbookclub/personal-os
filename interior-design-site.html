Philosophy

Good design is
slow to arrive
and slow to age.

We take between eight months and two years on any given project. Not because we are slow — because precision at scale requires time. Every material decision, every proportion, every threshold between spaces is deliberated until it resolves into something that could not be otherwise.

Our references are not last season’s design fairs. They are the Alhambra. Carlo Scarpa’s Castelvecchio. The way Donald Judd arranged his boxes in Marfa. We are interested in permanence — buildings that age well and rooms that deepen with use.

— Margot Leclerc
Principal & Founder

Services

01 — Residential

Private Homes
& Apartments

We design primary residences and secondary homes for clients who understand that a well-made interior is not decoration but infrastructure — something you inhabit every day for decades.

  • Full gut renovation and ground-up
  • Material selection and custom millwork
  • Art placement and lighting strategy
  • Landscape and threshold design

02 — Hospitality

Hotels &
Private Clubs

Hospitality spaces must be legible on first entry and rewarding over time. We have designed boutique hotels, members’ clubs, and private dining environments across Europe and North America.

  • Concept and spatial narrative
  • FF&E specification and procurement
  • Acoustic and lighting design
  • Brand environment integration

03 — Workspace

Offices &
Studios

The best workplaces are not amenity-driven but attention-driven — spaces calibrated for focus, not distraction. We have worked with independent studios, law practices, and creative firms.

  • Space planning and programming
  • Acoustic and natural light optimization
  • Custom built-ins and storage
  • Technology integration

The
Studio

Forma was founded in 2012 by Margot Leclerc after a decade working with Tadao Ando and Herzog & de Meuron. The studio operates from offices in Paris and New York, with eleven architects and designers drawn from across Europe and the Americas.

We take on between four and seven projects each year. We are not interested in producing at volume. We are interested in producing work that holds up under scrutiny over thirty years.
